# Inviting a reviewer

You can ask someone to read a story and leave comments before it is finished. They follow a link you send them; they do not need an account.

## Creating a review link

Open the story's settings and find the Review section. Create a review link, optionally noting who it is for and how long it should work. Copy the link right away - it is shown only once - and send it however you like.

Each link is its own invitation. If you want feedback from three people, make three links, so you can see who said what and close them separately.

## What the reviewer sees

The link opens the story as one read-only manuscript. The reviewer gives a name (or, if they have an account here and are signed in, their own name is used), then reads and comments:

- Selecting a passage lets them comment on exactly those words.
- They can also comment on a whole scene.
- They see earlier comments on the story and can reply to them.

Reviewers only ever see this one story. They cannot edit your text, and they cannot see your other stories, characters, or notes.

## Working through the feedback

Open "See the feedback" in the Review section (or visit the story's review page) to read every thread. Reply to keep a conversation going, and resolve a thread when you have dealt with it. Resolved threads stay visible for the record, and you can reopen them.

Comments point at the exact words they were made on. If you edit the text afterwards, the comment follows the passage when it can; if the passage itself was rewritten, the comment is kept and marked so you know it referred to older text.

## Ending a review

Revoke a link in the Review section to stop it working. The comments you already received stay. Deleting the story removes its review threads and reviewers with it.
