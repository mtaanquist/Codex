# Planning your world

The planning view holds everything your stories draw on: characters, places, and lore. These belong to the universe, so every story in it can use them.

## Characters, places, and lore

Create an entry and give it a name and a short summary. The summary is what shows when someone hovers a mention in the editor. Add longer notes in the body.

Lore entries are grouped into categories you define, such as "Factions" or "Magic". A category's colour is the colour its entries are underlined in.

## Aliases

Give a character their nicknames and variants as aliases. The editor uses them to detect mentions, so "the Captain" can point at the same person as her name.

## The outline

Each story has its own outline, separate from the chapters you have drafted. Use it to plan what happens before or instead of writing it straight through. An outline point can link to the scene that realises it.

## Relationships

You can record how entities relate: who is whose parent, which faction holds which city. Pick a relationship type and add a note; the reverse shows on the other entry's page automatically.

## Bringing entities into a story

An entity joins a story when you create it while working in that story, or when you add it by hand. The story's plan then shows just the characters, places, and lore that belong to it.
