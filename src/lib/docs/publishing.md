# Publishing your work

Publishing turns a finished story into a public reading page that anyone with the link can read. It never exposes your drafts: publishing takes a frozen snapshot, and the reading pages only ever show that snapshot. You keep writing; the public edition stays as it was until you publish again.

## Before you can publish

An administrator enables publishing for your account. Once it is on, claim a handle on your account page, under Profile. Your handle is the address your public pages live at, and it is permanent, so choose it with care.

## Publishing a story

Open the story's settings and publish it. You can give the edition a label, such as "First edition". Publishing again replaces the public version with a fresh snapshot of your current text.

## Visibility

Each story has a visibility:

- **Private** keeps it to yourself.
- **Unlisted** is reachable by direct link but is not listed on your public page.
- **Public** is listed on your page at your handle.

## Your public page

When your profile is set to public, your handle page shows your name, a short bio, any links you add, and your listed stories. You control all of that on your account page, under Profile.

## Taking something down

Set a story back to private, or republish after edits. An administrator can also take an edition down if needed.
